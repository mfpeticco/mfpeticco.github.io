Caltech Academics

Here is a sample of the relevant coursework I have completed:

  • ME72ab - Capstone Design Competition: A 2-term class where ~40 students competed in teams of 6-7 to build and fight 3 sumo robots each. Learn more on my projects page!
  • ME133ab - Robotics: Forward/inverse kinematics, dynamics, ROS/Gazebo, and pathfinding/planning algorithms.
  • AE105abc - Space Engineering: Orbital mechanics, systems engineering, mission design, and proposals
  • ME 40 - Dimensional and Data Analyses in Engineering
  • ME 14 - Design and Fabrication: GD&T, design of a single-speed transmission, and arduino mechatronics projects.
  • ME 13 - Mechanical Prototyping: Fabricating with lathes, mills, waterjets, 3D printers, and laser cutters.
  • Ma 2 - Differential Equations
  • ME 12abc - Mechanics (Statics & Dynamics)
  • ME 11abc - Thermal Science & Fluid Dynamics
  • CS 1 - Introduction to Computer Programming
  • Ma 1abc - Single- & Multi-Variable Calculus (Proof-based), Linear Algebra
  • Ph 1abc - Classical Mechanics & Electromagnetism

At Caltech, I am president of the California Beta chapter of Tau Beta Pi, the oldest engineering honor society in the United States. I have also been a teaching assistant for several classes, which you can read about on my Work Experience page

SUNY & NFA Academics

During high school, I was in program known as Newburgh PTECH (Formerly Excelsior Academy), a Pathways In Technology Early College Highschool (PTECH) program that was formed in partnership with Newburgh Free Academy (NFA), IBM, and SUNY Orange. This program allows students to obtain both a high school diploma from NFA and an Associate's Degree in Applied Science in either Networking or Cyber Security from SUNY Orange. These classes were taken at SUNY Orange Middletown Campus alongside fellow SUNY students. I am very content with the challenging and fascinating coursework in Cybersecurity I was given the opportunity to take.
