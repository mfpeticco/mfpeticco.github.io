Excelsior Robotics

During my first year of high school, I found that one of our teachers was fascilitating a new robotics team. After participating in the FIRST Tech Challenge that year, one of my friends and I were name co-fascilitators for our sophmore robotics team. We did well in competition considering the lack of financial resources and time. Scheduling complications lead to the inability for robotics to be fascilitated during my junior year, so this year I plan to bring robotics back with a new focus on building combat robots.

Combat Robotics

Combat Robotics is a sport that involves builders using all aspects of STEM to build robots that are capable of combat with one another. The goal is simple: In match that lasts less than three minutes, disable your opponent through a knockout or by judge's decision. Robots can range from as light as 150 gram "fairyweights" to 340 pound "superheavyweight". I became interested after being introduced to the TV show "Battlebots" by my dad during my freshman year, but I have only recently decided to build my own. I'm currently building a 12 pound "hobbyweight" robot for fun but plan on building a more serious 3 pound robot later this year.

Currently, I am working on a combat robot that is a replica of the 2018 BattleBots entry, "Tombstone". I shall name it "Tombclone".

The frame will be made out 1/2" diameter steel tubing.

The two drive motors will be two of these modified drill motors I got for 5$ at a flea market.

I used this project as an opporunity to learn stick welding. I had always wanted to learn how to weld but because I am in the PTECH high school program (which you can read about over on my academics page) I did not have the opportunity due to scheduling issues.

The basic shape of the frame completed. All the electronics and other hardware need mounting points, but the basic shape is done. Hand for scale.
