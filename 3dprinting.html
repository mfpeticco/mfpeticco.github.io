3D Printing and Designing

From a thought to a physical object in a matter of hours

The Goal

In 8th grade, I first learned about 3d printing from my librarian. She handed me the first 3d model I had ever seen or touched, a small gyroscope model. The fact that something could go from an idea to a 3d model to a physical object was amazing, and I had always wanted to do it myself. Once I got to high school I had access to some basic 3d printers. I was upset, however, that I could never tinker with them and improve them as they were school property. So, I saved up over the course of a few months and bought a cheap 200$ 3d printer from a random vendor in China. I still use it to this day and the amount of modifications I made to it allow it to print like a 3d printer worth 4 times its price.

Here's a few notes about my 3d printing:

  1. I use Fusion 360 to create my CAD models, or Thingiverse for community-made models.
  2. For slicing the model (convering the 3d model into machine language the 3d printer can understand) I use Cura, though soon I want to update to Simplify3D (it is NOT a cheap program, though).
  3. My entire 2018 internship at IBM revolved around 3d printing. Check it out on my Internship page.
  4. I use 3d printing in almost all of my projects, such as my Portable Wii project and my Combat Robot.
